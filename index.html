Project Overview

This project compiles experimental and computational insights across selected phosphide semiconductor families relevant to optoelectronic and photovoltaic applications. The goal of the database is to create a consistent platform for comparing crystal chemistry, optical response, band-gap trends, and photoluminescence behavior across structurally distinct material groups, while also documenting the experimental conditions used for measurement.

About the Database

Each material-family page is intended to gather crystal structure visuals, band-gap information, representative PL data, and literature references into a unified format for quick comparison. A separate PL settings page documents measurement parameters such as excitation wavelength, objective, filter, spot size, and temperature, keeping experimental conditions distinct from the materials overview.

Data & Attribution

This database compiles experimental and computational insights across selected phosphide semiconductor families. The computational work is the result of a high-throughput screening of ~40,000 materials using first-principles DFT calculations with the HSE06 functional performed by Zhenkun Yuan. All photoluminescence measurements presented here were performed and analyzed by Smitakshi Goswami as part of ongoing research on next-generation photovoltaic and infrared materials. The BaCd₂P₂ PL measurements were performed by Gideon Kassa.

This materials database was developed and curated by Smitakshi Goswami.
